A quick rant: a while ago, Google Pages suddenly became inaccessible. The reason, of course, is that line from Benshan: "Who uses it knows."

In China, when you can't open a webpage, the first thought isn't "my network is having issues" but "has this site been harmonized?" Sigh...

While I don't fully agree with this practice (harmonizing sites trying to use .xxx domains is still necessary), I think the authorities could maintain a public list telling people which sites have been harmonized and why. That way, next time a page won't open, people won't start wondering about the content...

Of course, this is just my humble opinion. As a PM (note: not Pocket Monster Pikachu, nor Project Manager), I will steadfastly adhere to: "If there are differing opinions regarding Party resolutions and policies, while resolutely implementing them, one may state reservations and submit opinions to the Party's higher organizations up to the Central Committee."

So, resolutely implement, resolutely implement.
