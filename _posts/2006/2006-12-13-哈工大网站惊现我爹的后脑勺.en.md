Today I was browsing HIT (Harbin Institute of Technology) news and accidentally clicked on an article about an engine donation. Seemed ordinary enough. Suddenly, a familiar back of a head caught my eye (red circle in the picture). That back of the head looked so familiar — where had I seen it before? A round head, layered neck fat, seamlessly integrated with the shoulders with barely any neck. Could it be...

<a href="http://images.blogcn.com/2006/12/13/11/omale,20061213213216.jpg" target="_blank"><img border="0" onload="if(this.width>screen.width/2)this.width=screen.width/2;" src="http://images.blogcn.com/2006/12/13/11/omale,20061213213216.jpg" /></a>

I read the whole article with suspicion, and sure enough, that back of the head belonged to none other than my dad! He was participating in a so-called "public welfare activity" and got snapped by a paparazzo reporter who posted it online.

My dad's really something — after all these years working in academia, my own photo has never appeared on a ".edu.cn" domain website, yet my dad easily got his "head" a spot. But HIT was really cheap — even though they didn't donate cash, they at least gave engines. Grind one down and you could claim "independently developed IP engine" and apply for major projects; or just scrap them and sell them off as "research funds." How did it end up with just a head, and the back of it at that? I immediately fired up the search engine and searched frantically. Finally found the "multi-pic-kill-cat" full version of the news.
