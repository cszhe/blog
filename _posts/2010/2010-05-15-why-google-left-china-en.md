Original story:

Google wanted to donate money to Chinese universities to set up Android labs. A good thing. But they insisted on following American procedures: verify the university's legal identity, submit business license, tax license, legal person certificate, legal person signature, bank account info &#8212; all in English. Holy crap. Who knows where Tongji University's business license is kept? Doesn't Google know Chinese leaders don't read English? You expect them to look up words in a dictionary? If you put a Chinese document in front of them and call them "sir," they might not even glance at it. Unless it contains a stack of cash. Getting Chinese universities to do this is Mission Impossible. So far, no school has succeeded. Tongji is no exception. Whether this money will ever enter the university is questionable.
