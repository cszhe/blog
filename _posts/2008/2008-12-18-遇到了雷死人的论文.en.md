It's that time of year again — master's thesis defense season. I've reviewed dozens of papers recently and my head is spinning. Many感慨. Sina reported a few days ago that China's academic paper output has surpassed the US. What's there to be happy about? Quantity over quality has always been our specialty and style, hasn't it? Can dozens or hundreds of Chinese papers equal one good foreign paper? More papers isn't necessarily a good thing, yet they tout it loudly. Sigh.

Master's students need to publish papers to graduate — this paper, that paper. Short papers are okay, clearly priced, paid for with money. But the big thesis gets dumped on the advisor. The writing is absolute garbage. What to do? Not let them graduate? Can't do that. But looking at the paper quality... I really don't know what to do. The big environment, the big environment. When will it improve?

Alright, let me share a student's Chinese and English abstracts — after three revisions. If this doesn't shock you, it's free. Best效果 when reading Chinese and English side by side.

**Abstract (Chinese)**

> Nowadays in the computer industry, it is a case of a hundred schools of thought contending and a hundred flowers blooming. The author believes that in the area of network communication, there will be relatively significant development. This paper systematically elaborates on real-time network communication. At the same time, considering the actual situation at this stage: although every organization connects to the Internet via network, due to practical work needs, only some employees who need it are allowed direct external network access, while most employees are only allowed internal network access. Therefore, what this paper discusses is real-time communication within a local area network.
>
> After carefully studying computer network programming, the author believes that simple access between just two computers is not actually difficult. Therefore, after synthesizing various current computer interconnection methods, the author used various methods to interconnect two or more computers, integrated the different methods in the final chapter, and finally presented a feature-rich real-time communication system. The methods include:
>
> 1. Socket-based real-time communication over TCP
> 2. Java RMI and CORBA interface-based real-time communication
> 3. Client/server Internet application-based real-time communication
> 4. Client/server multicast-based real-time communication
> 5. Servlet multi-threading and Applet multi-threading-based real-time communication
> 6. Browser/server-based real-time communication
>
> This paper uses various mainstream and non-mainstream interconnection methods, different interfaces, different network technologies, and different network interconnection schemes to connect computers — from simple DOS-era serial and parallel port interconnection, to Windows Socket interconnection, to CORBA and RMI technology, and even web-based JSP technology interconnection — providing users with optimal interconnection solutions from different angles and technologies.
>
> **Keywords:** Network, computer, communication, real-time communication tool
