Since 2002, my blog has been hosted on blogcn, because back then there were relatively few BSPs (not board support packages) to choose from. Seven years have passed, times have changed, and blogging has long become part of many netizens' daily lives. But recently blogcn has been almost inaccessible. The servers are in Hangzhou — how could this happen?

I can't take it anymore. Moving! I applied for this blog a few years ago but never used it — it was actually for testing my wife's blog. Now it feels pretty good. Fortunately, Blogbus's migration service was quite thorough. The move was done quickly. From now on, this will be my base. I'll write more technical articles. The main reason I didn't want to write technical articles before was because of the terrible blogcn service...

I wanted to post a closure announcement on my old blog, but now I can't access it. So be it.
